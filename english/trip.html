Welcome to Merida!

The reception will take place in the Hacienda Dzibikak which is located approximately 40 minutes away from Merida, so we highly recommend to find lodging in that city. Merida is the capital of the state of Yucatan and is one of the most popular destinations in Mexico. Its cultural, architectural, historical, archeological and social richness are impressive. It has been named "Cultural Capital of the Americas" two years in a row due to the enormous variety of activities and cultural evens that happen throughout the year.

Michelle's maternal family is originally from Yucatan so we have a special connection with the city. The heart is where the family is, but objectively, Yucatan has thousands of attractions for all ages and taste: great party, beach, culture, eco-tourism, and more. You can eat seafood, go to a cenote, visit an hacienda, museums, hike the archeological sites, explore the fauna which ranges from exotic birds to crocodiles, attend a dance show, or simply drink a margarita in one of downtown's terrace. Of all of these wonders, one that we particularly enjoy is the local cuisine. Michelle is willing to fight for the statement that yucatenca food is the best cuisine of the country and Jonathan has yucatecan pork as one of his favorite dishes. We challenge you to confirm or deny these declarations. Every dish has a very specific and special story and essence: you need to eat panuchos, salbutes, vaporcitos, chayitas, sopa de lima, cochinita, but negro, papadzules, queso relleno, poc-chuc, pan de cazón, marquesitas... and basically everything that you find on your way! This is just a small selection ;)

Beyond the food, Merida is magical and charming. Its people is very particular, its structure is gorgeous and the vibe is just marvellous. The city is very safe and has multiple attractions that are worth all the effort. Walk, discover and get enchanted with all the things that the city has to offer. Just protect yourself from the heat... and the mosquitoes.

SPECIAL NOTE: Remember that 1 USD DOLLAR is **approximately** equal to 18 MXN PESOS.

Transportation

Vía Cancún: There are multiple direct (and relatively cheap) routes from many foreign cities to Cancun. When you arrive to Cancun airport you have three options:1) Take an ADO bus directly from the airport to several stops in Merida including hotels and bus stations (Approximate travelling time: 4 hours, Cost: $640 MXN pesos), 2) Take a bus from Cancun' downtown (Approximate travelling time: 4 hours, Cost: $226 MXN pesos), o 3) Rent a car and drive to Mérida.

Vía Mérida: Probably the easiest option. Flights arrive to Aeropuerto Internacional Manuel Crescencio Rejón (Clave: MID), and from there you can get to your hotel via taxi o Uber.

Tips

Recommendations for foodies and people that love eating... (a few of the one million options that are available)

  • Taqueria Nuevo San Fernando: Yucatecan pork and piglet baguettes and tacos.
  • La chaya maya: regional cuisine and dishes. Hand-made tortillas and great price-quality relation.
  • Los almendros: a region classic. Traditional dishes with a light upscale touch. Very touristic but good.
  • La terraza del norte: panuchos, salbutes y lime soup
  • Rosas y Xocolate: for more elegant taste. It is inside a hotel boutique.
  • Crabster: if you plan to go to the beach this is an awesome place in Progreso where you can eat excellent seafood with a modern touch.
  • La tradición: another classic. Unbeatable Yucatecan dishes.
  • Pistache: if you are already tired of the local cuisine (sometimes it happens),this is a great place to have French-style breakfast. The bakery is amazing: freshly baked bread, cake, macarons and cookies.
  • Doña Evelia: in Motul , an hour from Merida, you can find this jewel. The "huevos motuleños" (eggs with ham, peas, tortilla and sauce), is a dish that has become incredibly famous through the years and was created by Doña Evelia in this small market. She still cooks the sauce for you, and they can change your life.
  • Helados El Colón: sherbet, champolas (milkshakes) and desserts. Don't miss the coconut, corn, mamey, guanabana and "mantecado" flavors.
  • Kinich: located in Izamal, an hour from Merida. This restaurant serves 100% Yucatecan food and is in the Top 3 of Michelle's favorite restaurants (worldwide). Everything is delicious. EVERYTHING. Honorific mention to the Dutch cheese and Mayan spinach empanadas, the papadzules with longaniza, the poc-chuc and the coconut ice-cream. It is worth every traveling second and sweat drop because of the heat (and Izamal is just wonderful). This is a perfect day trip!
  • Apoala: food and drinks menu is creative and good. There are pretty fancy drinks based on mezcal and fruits, and the dishes are not limited to Yucatecan cuisine. The best moment to go is thursday night while the "Serenata de Santa Lucia" (trova serenade) is happening exactly in the park where the restaurant is. Arrive early to get a nice table and enjoy music and food.
  • Other non-Yucatecan options:: if you are a bit fed up with local cuisine, try La Tratto, La Recova, Trotter's, Brayan's, Néctar, si ya se hartaron de la fritanga, prueben La Tratto, La Recova, Trotters, Oliva o Brayans.

To visit: If you are interested in visiting any of these places, let us know. We will do our best to arrange group visits and give you advice.

  • Eating Is a Thing to Do in Merida: Merida is home to some of the best food throughout the Yucatan and Central America. So be sure to try the local cuisine which has some huge regional differences compared to elsewhere in Mexico.
  • Go for a Bike Ride on Sunday Morning: Bici-Ruta Merida
  • Dancing in the Streets of Merida During its Weekly Fiestas!: vaqueria Night on Calle 62 at Plaza Grande begins at 9:00 pm. Ballet Folkloric Ballet of Merida accompanied by the Jaranera Orchestra (Mondays), Musical Memories in Parque Santiago begins at 8:30. It attracts an older crowd who come out to dance to Big Band sounds played by the live orchestra (Tuesdays), Yucatecan Serenade in Parque Santa Lucia at 9:00pm is one of the longest standing weekly events in the city having been going on for over 40 years. This is a must for things to do in Merida on a Thursday night (Thursdays), stroll up Calle 60 and hang a right towards the beginning of the Paseo. On Calle 46a, between Calles 47 & 49, you can find Noche Mexicana (Mexican Night) starting a 8:00pm, which boasts Mexican culture from throughout the country through traditional song and dance performances (Saturdays).
  • On weekend nights a few of the main streets in the city are closed to traffic and the city becomes abuzz. Sidewalk cafes begin setting up in the streets. Stages are erected in the plazas and free concerts take place, both traditional festive song & dance as well as modern pop music. Street performers also come out to play. Street food vendors don’t hesitate to get into the mix as well, providing plenty of opportunities for a midnight snack. Salsa music begins blaring from a bar and people in the streets tend to spontaneously break into dance. Children run around carelessly as there parents are somewhere nearby enjoying the atmosphere.
  • Visit and take a tour of the Palacio the Gobierno, Palacio Municipal, Catedral de San Idelfonso, Museo MACAY... all in downtown!
  • Visit Casa de Montejo: Want to know what wealthy Victorian Mexican life was like back in the hacienda days? Wander on into Casa de Montejo on the Plaza’s south side to take a peak at the rooms, furniture, and fine china of this former Meridian mansion.
  • How Fun! Cantinas: Drinking Your Way Across Merida: There are lots of great cantinas and lively bars to enjoy around town. Happy hour runs late here so you can often find great drink deals into the 8:00 hour and beyond. Whether your after cervezas, margaritas, palomas, or micheladas, there’s no shortage of libations to be had around town. There are even some local craft beers made in Merida and around the Yucatan that you can only find in this area. Move over Corona!
  • Have a Nice Sit in Plaza Grande: If you’re searching for what to do in Merida, don’t overlook just taking a break in the center of town to absorb all the beautiful ambiance. The Plaza Grande is the perfect place for that. It’s a great central spot to simply take a break and unwind. This is where many Meridians and tourists alike congregate during the day, into the evening, and particularly on the weekends. The Catedral de San Ildefonso and other grand architecture surround this town square to provide a beautiful 360-degree backdrop. Enjoy the company you’re with or practice your Spanish and make some new friends. The city’s signature dual chairs facing themselves side-by-side make it easy to chat with one another.
  • Explore Merida by Horse and Carriage: Skip the double decker hop-on hop-off tour bus and instead tour Merida by horse & carriage. A friendly guide will lead you through the city center and up the historic Paseo de Montejo for a reasonable price.
  • Cruise the Paseo de Montejo: You can’t come to Merida without cruising up the grand Paseo de Montejo. This wide avenue built in Merida’s heyday conjectures images of Paris’s Champs-Élysées. Historic mansions line the grand avenue, as do quaint cafes. Pop into one and sip on a coffee. Or take in some history while strolling the Paseo. Why not both? Consider popping into the Museo Regional de Arquaeología de Yucatán, among other Merida attractions. Just look for the big pink mansion on the East side of the Paseo.
  • Experience Pok Ta Pok: Representation of the Mayan Ball Game: The tradition of this ancient sport is kept alive each Friday night at 8:00 pm on Calle 60 in front of the Cathedral and Plaza Grande. Watch the players battle it out as traditional live music accompanies the spectacle. Although this game is played as a recreation for demonstrative purposes, the players still seem to be very much into the game with a high competitive spirit.
  • Get Lost Amongst Chaos in the Lucas de Galvez Market Merida: Merida’s big market is a site to be experienced. You can find fresh vegetables, homemade crafts, local clothing, a new pet, and everything in between. This vibrant and bustling market can get crowded. It’s packed with locals and that’s all part of the fun. Weave your way through the hectic maze-like atmosphere and score a bargain.
  • Take the Free Walking Tour: Every morning there is a free walking tour in the Plaza Grande in Merida centro, which provides for a great lesson in the city’s history while also providing you bearings to know your way around town. The free walking tour of Merida is a great introduction of the central Merida attractions all around the plaza. You’ll learn a lot about the city itself, as the walking tour is full of many interesting facts that you may not have read up on before you arrived to town.
  • Visit the Gran Museo del Mundo Maya : This is an impressive museum with tons of exhibitions from which to learn everything about Mayan Culture since the beginning (the infamous meteorite that ended the Jurassic area), to more contemporary times.
  • Visit nearby cenotes such as the three cenotes of Cuzuma, visited by horse cart!
  • Go see wild flamingos in Celestun or on the Rio Lagartos
  • Tour an old hacienda outside of town
  • Take this highly reviewed Chichen Itza Day Trip from Merida
  • Try a tour to Uxmal and Kabah, to see the Yucatan’s other famous ruins,
  • Explore the lesser visited Mayapan Ruins or Dzibilchaltún
  • Journey to the nearby picturesque Mayan town of Izamal
  • Take a road-trip down the Ruta Puuc
  • Simply have a nice lazy day at the beach in Progreso, only about 30 minutes away by car.
